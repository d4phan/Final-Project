Are Temperate Coastal Zones Entering a New Era of "Dry Heat"?

Explore how climate change is reshaping the coasts through interactive data visualization.

What is "Dry Heat"?

Dry heat means high temperatures with little humidity or rainfall. Historically rare on coasts, but is that changing?

Historical vs. Predicted Data (1850-2100)

Notice the sharp rise in temperature after 2000, with precipitation staying nearly flat. Predicted data (2025-2100) is essential to understand future climate trajectories.

Regional Focus: Southern California & East Coast

Southern California Coast is projected to enter a "dry heat" era by 2100 with >3°C warming and minimal precipitation change.

Conclusion

Some temperate coastal zones are indeed entering a new era of "dry heat"—especially in the American West. This has major implications for ecosystems, water resources, and human communities.
